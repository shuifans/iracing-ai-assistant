# Getting Started with iRacing

Congratulations on becoming a member with us here at iRacing.com! This guide is designed to help introduce you to the service, and, most importantly, to get you on the track. Throughout this series, we'll walk you through everything that iRacing has to offer. Soon, you'll feel at home among the best online community in the sim racing world.

## System Requirements

iRacing is designed for everyone. If you have a relatively new PC you should have no problem signing up for iRacing.com. You can also run iRacing on a Mac using a Windows emulator.

iRacing requires a controller to enjoy the full scope of racing experiences offered by the team. Explore the [system requirements](https://iracing.com/system-requirements) page to learn more about recommended controllers, steering wheels, foot pedals, gamepads, joysticks and more.

## Membership

Once you've determined whether or not you have the proper hardware and software for iRacing, you can easily sign up immediately. When you join iRacing, you can choose from a variety of affordable, full-featured plans to help meet your specific budget and needs. iRacing memberships range from one month to two years at great, low prices.

## Leagues & Official Series

Once you've got a grip on the game, you'll likely want to sign up for league racing. Learn about [leagues](https://iracing.com/leagues) and [official racing series](https://iracing.com/series) for more.
